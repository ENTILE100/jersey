# JerseyDek Growth Cookbook

A simple playbook to grow your jersey marketplace in Thailand. Focus: get your first sellers and buyers, then grow with social media.

The hardest part of any marketplace is the "empty room" problem: buyers won't come if there's nothing to buy, and sellers won't post if there are no buyers. So your job at the start is to **fake the fullness** — fill the site yourself, then bring people in.

---

## Part 1 — Get your first 100 users

### Step 1: Seed the site yourself (Week 1)
Before you tell anyone, the site must look alive.

- List 20–30 real jerseys yourself. Use your own, friends', or ones you photograph at markets (with permission).
- Take clean photos: plain background, good light, front and back.
- Price them realistically. Check Facebook groups and Shopee for what jerseys actually sell for.
- Goal: a new visitor should see a full, busy page — never "No jerseys yet."

### Step 2: Go where jersey buyers already are
You don't need new people. You need to reach people who already buy and sell university jerseys.

- **Facebook groups** are the #1 place in Thailand. Search for: "เสื้อช็อปมหาลัย", "ขายเสื้อช็อป", "เสื้อเชียร์มหาลัย", plus each big university's buy/sell group (Chula, TU, KU, MU, etc.).
- Join 15–20 of these groups.
- Don't spam. Post helpful things: "I made a free site just for university jerseys — easier to search by uni and size. Posting mine here too." Add a photo and your link.

### Step 3: Start at ONE university
Don't try to cover all of Thailand at once. Pick one big university with strong jersey culture (e.g. Chula or Thammasat). Own it completely.

- Post in that university's groups every few days.
- Offer the first 20 sellers there something: free "featured" placement, or you personally help photograph and list their jersey.
- When one university feels active, copy the exact same playbook to the next one.

### Step 4: Use the faculty/club structure
Thai university jerseys are tied to faculties (คณะ) and student years. That's your growth map.

- Reach out to faculty student committees and club leaders. They run group chats with hundreds of students.
- Offer: "List your faculty's leftover jerseys here, free." Leftover stock from events is gold.

### Step 5: Make every seller bring a buyer
When someone lists a jersey, ask them to share their listing link to their own friends/story. Give them a reason: "Listings shared to story sell 2x faster." Each seller becomes a tiny marketing channel.

### Quick targets
- Week 1: 30 listings (you), 5 real sellers.
- Month 1: 1 university active, 50 sellers, first 10 real sales.
- Month 3: 3 universities, listings come in without you asking.

## Part 2 — Marketing & social media

Your buyers are young, on their phones, and on TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook. Show the product, don't "advertise."

### TikTok (your biggest opportunity)
Short video is the cheapest way to reach Thai students.

- **"Jersey of the day"**: 10–15 sec clip showing one cool jersey + price + "link in bio."
- **Restock / new drop** videos: fast cuts of 5 new listings with trendy sound.
- **Storytime / nostalgia**: "POV: you find your old faculty jersey for sale." Emotion sells jerseys.
- Post daily. Most won't go viral — that's normal. One hit brings hundreds of users.
- Always end with a clear action: "Search [name] or tap the link."

### Instagram
- Treat your grid like a clean catalog: one jersey per post, same photo style.
- Use Stories for daily new listings + "sold" stickers (sold stickers build trust and FOMO).
- Reels: repost your TikToks here.

### Facebook
- This is where the actual buyers/sellers are in Thailand. Keep posting in groups.
- Make your own Facebook Page and post every listing.
- Consider a small ad budget later (฿100–200/day) targeting students near one university.

### Content rules that work
1. **Show the jersey, not your logo.** People come for product.
2. **Always show the price.** Hidden prices kill interest.
3. **Post often and consistently** beats posting "perfect" rarely.
4. **Reuse everything**: one photo shoot → website listing + TikTok + IG + FB.
5. **Reply fast.** Speed of reply is the difference between a sale and a lost buyer.

### Cheap growth tricks
- **Micro-creators**: ask student TikTokers (5k–50k followers) to show your site for a free jersey. Cheaper and more trusted than ads.
- **Campus events**: jersey sale season (freshman week, sports days) is your peak. Plan posts around the school calendar.
- **Referral**: "List 1 jersey, get featured for free" or "Bring a friend who lists, both get featured."

---

## Part 3 — Build trust (or growth dies)

Marketplaces live and die on trust. A few scams early can kill your reputation.

- Tell users to **meet in safe public places** and **check the jersey before paying** (already shown on each listing page).
- Add seller reviews/ratings as soon as you can.
- Remove fake or scammy listings fast.
- Be a real human in comments and DMs — early on, you ARE the brand.

---

## The one-line strategy
Fill the site yourself → dominate one university → turn every seller into a promoter → feed TikTok/IG/FB daily → protect trust. Repeat per university.
